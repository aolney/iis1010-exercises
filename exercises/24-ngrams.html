N-grams

We're going to use Green Eggs and Ham again because it is short and the repeated words make the probabilities less sparse.

This is a multi-step exercise. Do the following in order:

  • Get the data block. This has the text of Green Eggs and Ham as a giant string.
  • Get Ex 1 block. This block will turn the text into sentences by 'splitting' the text to make an array
    • Fill in the blank with the character to split on. What's at the end of a sentence?
    • Run it. Notice we now have an array of sentences!
    • Notice there is some white space before/after the sentences. We'll fix that.
  • Get Ex 2 block. This will "pad" the sentences with special characters to mark the beginning/end of a sentence
    • Fill in the blanks with S and E. Make sure you have whitespace between them and the first/last word in the sentence.
    • Run it. Notice the padding!
    • Notice we fixed the whitespace from before with 'trim()'
  • Get Ex 3 block. This block will make a map of bigram frequencies and unigram frequencies we'll use to make probabilities.
    • Fill in the blank for 'split'. We want to break up each sentence into an array of words. What comes between words?
    • Fill in the blanks for increasing the frequency maps. If we haven't seen a unigram/bigram before, and we've just seen it, what should it's frequency be? How much should we increase it every time we see it?
    • Run it and scroll up/down. Notice we now have frequency maps for unigrams and bigrams. Look for some of the most frequent of each. Do they make sense?
  • Get Ex 4 block. This block gets the bigram log probability and probability for a string of words.
    • Fill in the blank for frequency. Remember we want to give unseen bigrams a count of 1.
    • Fill in the blank for logProbability. Notice its a sum!
    • Fill in the GetProbabilities at the bottom with the test words given. You can type them in even though the "()" looks green. Just click there and type.
    • Run it.
    • Do the probabilities make sense? Which one is bigger? Which test words seem more common?
  • Get Ex 5 block. This block gets a sequence of next words given a starting word.
    • Fill in the blanks for GetNextWordMax and GetNextWordRandom; both have blanks in their return values. What should each be returning (think of the name of the function).
    • Fill in the blank "f" in GetNextWords with GetNextWordMax.
    • Fill in the blank for the starting word when you call "GetNextWords"
    • Run it! Look at the probability for the next word (long row). Notice that it is always choosing the max. That must be good, right? Scroll to the bottom and look at the words output. Notice that it's repeating the same words over and over. Max is like giving everything else zero probability. No generalizability!
    • Switch the previous "f" in GetNextWords to GetNextWordRandom.
    • Run it and look at the output. Now we are choosing low probability words sometimes. Scroll down and look at the words output. Notice that it's not very grammatical. Looks like giving 1 to unseen bigrams might be too much (overgeneraliztion)!
    • One last go - go up to where you gave unseen bigrams a frequency of 1 and change to .01. Now repeat the previous steps. Looks more reasonable?
